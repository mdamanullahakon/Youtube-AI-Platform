## 3. FULL CINEMATIC STORY

---

### PART ONE: THE INVITATION

The flyer was taped to a lamp post near campus.

*"Clinical Sleep Study — $4,000 — 7 Nights — Free Lodging & Meals — St. Mary's Auxiliary Wing."*

I was behind on rent. My mother had just been diagnosed. I needed the money in a way that made the fine print irrelevant.

I called the number. A woman answered on the first ring.

*"St. Mary's Sleep Research. How did you hear about us?"*

Her voice was pleasant. Professional. But there was something rehearsed about it — like she had said those exact words thousands of times.

I told her about the flyer.

*"Ah. Yes. We just put those up."*

A pause.

*"What's your name?"*

I told her.

*"When can you start?"*

No screening questions. No medical history. No intake forms.

I should have hung up.

---

The building was at the edge of the city — an old Catholic hospital that had been partially decommissioned. The main doors were locked. A side entrance had a sign: *"St. Mary's Sleep Research — Ring for Entry."*

I rang.

The same woman opened the door. She was in her fifties. Gray hair pulled tight. Glasses. A white coat. She smiled — but her eyes didn't move.

*"You must be Daniel. Come in. We've been expecting you."*

She led me down a hallway. The lights were off. The only illumination came from emergency exit signs — red pools that made everyone look injured.

*"The other subjects are already here. You're our fourth."*

Subject four.

The dream came back to me.

---

### PART TWO: THE OTHER SUBJECTS

There were three others.

A girl named Maya — early twenties, quiet, kept her arms wrapped around herself. She looked like she hadn't slept in days before arriving.

An older man named Frank — late forties, told me he was a truck driver, said the money was "too good to pass up."

And a boy. Maybe nineteen. He didn't tell us his name. He sat in the corner of the common room and stared at the wall.

I later learned his name was Ethan.

I later learned he hadn't spoken since Night 3.

---

The rules were simple:

1. You sleep in your assigned room from 10 PM to 7 AM.
2. You wear a wrist monitor at all times.
3. You do not leave your room after lights out.
4. You do not attempt to observe other subjects.
5. You do not open your door for any reason between 10 PM and 7 AM.

*"If you hear anything outside your room,"* the woman said — her name was Dr. Kessler — *"ignore it. The building is old. It makes sounds. Do not open your door under any circumstances."*

Frank laughed.

*"What are you gonna do, lock us in?"*

Dr. Kessler looked at him.

*"Yes."*

She wasn't joking.

---

### PART THREE: NIGHT ONE

My room was exactly like the one in my dream.

Concrete walls. A single mattress on the floor. A light bulb in a wire cage that flickered constantly.

There was no window.

The door had a small observation panel — the kind you see in psychiatric units. A metal cover slid over it from the outside.

Right before lights out, I heard the metal cover slide shut.

Click.

I was in the dark.

---

The first night was uneventful.

I lay in the dark and listened to the building settle. Pipes creaking. Water dripping somewhere. The hum of electricity.

I fell asleep eventually.

I dreamed about Room 4 again.

This time, the voice was different.

*"Subject 4. Eyes open. Subject 4. Do not close your eyes. Subject 4. She is not your mother."*

I woke up gasping.

---

### PART FOUR: NIGHT TWO

Something changed on the second night.

I woke up at 2:14 AM to the sound of footsteps.

Slow. Deliberate. Walking down the hallway outside my room.

Then stopping.

Right outside my door.

I held my breath.

A knock.

Three slow knocks.

And then a voice — Dr. Kessler's voice — but wrong. Like a recording played slightly too slow.

*"Subject 4. Please state your name for the record."*

I didn't answer.

*"Subject 4. Please state your name for the record."*

I stayed silent.

The voice dropped an octave.

*"Subject 4. You are not the only one in that room."*

---

I scrambled to the door and pressed my eye to the observation panel.

There was a gap between the metal cover and the door frame — maybe a quarter of an inch.

I could see a sliver of the hallway.

It was dark. But I could make out a shape standing directly on the other side of the door.

It was wearing a white coat.

But it was too tall.

The shoulders were too high. The head was angled — not tilted, but *displaced*, like it had been attached incorrectly.

It wasn't Dr. Kessler.

It was wearing her skin.

---

### PART FIVE: THE OTHER SUBJECTS BREAK

The next morning, Ethan wasn't at breakfast.

Maya was shaking. She kept looking at the hallway.

Frank tried to open the main door. It was locked from the outside.

*"This is a fire hazard!"* he shouted.

Dr. Kessler appeared at the end of the hallway.

*"There is no fire, Mr. Caldwell."*

*"Where's the boy? Where's Ethan?"*

Dr. Kessler tilted her head.

*"Ethan is in observation. He had a difficult night. He will be returned to you when he is... calmer."*

She smiled.

Frank punched the wall.

*"We're leaving."*

*"You signed a contract, Mr. Caldwell. You agreed to seven nights. If you leave, you forfeit compensation. And..."* she paused. *"...you'll need to complete a mandatory psychiatric hold. For your safety."*

She held up a clipboard.

*"The paperwork has already been signed."*

---

### PART SIX: NIGHT THREE

I didn't want to sleep.

But at 11 PM, the lights went out. The metal cover slid shut. I was in the dark again.

I sat on the mattress and stared at the door.

At 1:23 AM, the footsteps came again.

This time, there were two sets.

They stopped outside my door.

Silence.

And then — a voice I recognized.

*"Daniel?"*

It was my mother.

*"Daniel, open the door. Why are you in there? Daniel, I'm scared. Open the door."*

Her voice was perfect. The exact cadence. The exact tone.

But my mother was 300 miles away.

And my mother had been dead for six months.

---

I didn't open the door.

But I screamed.

I screamed for help. I screamed for Frank and Maya. I screamed until my throat tore.

No one came.

And outside my door, my dead mother kept knocking.

*"Daniel, please. It's dark out here. Daniel, don't leave me alone. You always left me alone. Even when I was dying. You were too busy. Too tired. Too scared. Open the door, Daniel. Let me in."*

I pressed my hands to my ears.

But her voice came from inside my head.

*"Let me in."*

---

### PART SEVEN: THE TRUTH

It ended on Night 5.

But not the way you think.

On the morning of Day 5, Dr. Kessler opened my door personally.

*"Get your things. The study is terminated."*

I stumbled into the hallway.

Frank was gone. Maya was gone. Ethan was gone.

Dr. Kessler led me to the main door without speaking.

I stopped.

*"Where are they?"*

She didn't turn around.

*"They broke protocol, Daniel. They opened their doors."*

*"What does that mean?"*

She opened the main door. Sunlight flooded in. I hadn't seen sunlight in five days.

*"It means they're still in the building. But they're no longer subjects."*

She looked at me.

*"You kept your door closed. That's why you get to leave."*

---

I walked out.

I stood on the sidewalk.

I called the police.

They searched the building.

They found Frank in Room 2. He was alive. He didn't speak. He didn't blink. He just sat on the mattress, staring at the wall.

They found Maya in Room 3. She was alive too. She was scratching the same word into the concrete floor over and over:

*"MINE MINE MINE MINE MINE"*

They never found Ethan.

They didn't find Room 4.

---

### PART EIGHT: THE FINAL DESCENT

That was six days ago.

I haven't slept since.

Every time I close my eyes, I see it.

Room 4.

The concrete walls. The bare mattress. The flickering light.

And in the corner — a shape. A thing that looks like Dr. Kessler, but isn't. A thing that has been in that room since before the hospital was built. A thing that the study was designed to feed.

They weren't studying sleep.

They were selecting subjects.

And I passed the test.

---

Last night, I woke up at 3:47 AM.

There was a knock at my apartment door.

Three slow knocks.

And a voice — my own voice — saying:

*"Subject 4. Eyes open. Subject 4. You cannot un-participate. Subject 4. The study is over. But I am not."*

I moved my sofa in front of the door.

I'm writing this from my bathroom.

The knocking stopped an hour ago.

But now I can hear it coming from inside the walls.

---

## 4. FINAL LINE

**NARRATOR (whisper intensity, slow, calm terror):**

"They told me the study was over.

But I think I'm still in Room 4.

I think I never left.

And I think — if you're reading this — you should check your door.

Because St. Mary's is still recruiting.

And they don't always ask for volunteers."
